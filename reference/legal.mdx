## Responsible use

PC Caster relays streams that your PC can already access in a browser — it does not unlock content that is otherwise inaccessible to you. Using it responsibly means:

- **Only access streams you are legally entitled to view.** This includes streams you have a subscription to, streams from services you are licensed to use, or content in the public domain.
- **Comply with website terms of service.** Many streaming sites prohibit automated access or re-broadcasting, even for personal use.
- **Comply with copyright law in your jurisdiction.** Laws around recording, redistribution, and retransmission vary by country.
- **Do not redistribute streams.** PC Caster is a point-to-point tool between your PC and your own TV on your own network.

## What PC Caster does NOT do

<AccordionGroup>

  <Accordion title="It does not store or record streams">
    PC Caster's local proxy passes stream data through in real time — segments are fetched from the CDN and forwarded to the Roku as they are requested. Nothing is written to disk. The proxy has no buffering or recording capability.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="It does not bypass DRM encryption">
    PC Caster only handles streams that are already playable in a plain browser without a DRM license. It replicates the HTTP headers (`Referer`, `Origin`) and TLS fingerprint that the browser would send — it does not decrypt Widevine, PlayReady, FairPlay, or any other DRM-encrypted content. If a stream requires a DRM license to decode, PC Caster cannot play it.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="It does not access geo-locked content">
    The proxy re-fetches streams from the same IP address as your PC. It does not use a VPN, proxy server, or any technique to circumvent geographic restrictions. If a stream is blocked in your country, PC Caster will not unblock it.
  </Accordion>

</AccordionGroup>
